> Identity.

- “anime Duolingo”
- “cyberpunk skin on flashcards”
- “generic gamified learning app”

It now has:
- atmosphere
- lore direction
- visual consistency
- thematic cohesion
- psychological fantasy

- tactical infiltration
- signal decoding

- dangerous communication systems

The app currently suffers from a major mismatch between:
- presentation depth
AND
- gameplay depth

A large portion of the experience still functions like:

# SECTION 1 - WHAT ALREADY WORKS EXTREMELY WELL

---

## Current State

The dark tactical cyber-Japanese aesthetic works very well.

Strong points:
- black/purple contrast
- neon accents
- minimal UI
- scanner aesthetics
- signal terminology
- tactical presentation

The UI already feels more premium than most indie educational apps.

It creates:
- mystery
- tension
- immersion
- operational atmosphere

---

## Why It Works

The UI avoids:
- childishness
- excessive color overload
- generic anime tropes
- loud gamification

Instead it feels:
- controlled
- classified
- cold
- dangerous
- technological

This is rare.

## Current State

The dungeon flow is one of the strongest systems.

Examples:
- PREP

- SECTOR
- BOSS

That shift is extremely important.

# SECTION 2 - CORE DESIGN PROBLEMS

---

# 2.1 The Biggest Problem

## The Core Issue

The app often feels like:

> Standard learning mechanics wearing a cyberpunk skin.

Instead of:

> A system fundamentally designed around signal infiltration gameplay.

This is the single most important issue.

---

# 2.2 Current Learning Loop

Most interactions still boil down to:
- read
- click
- continue

Even though presentation is excellent.

---

# 2.3 What Needs To Happen

The mechanics themselves must evolve.

The gameplay should feel like:
- decoding
- intercepting
- reconstructing
- surviving unstable communication
- identifying corrupted language
- reacting under pressure

NOT:
- static quiz selection

- tension

### Pressure Mechanics

- interrupted dialogue

### Tactical Mechanics
Examples:
- choose which transmission to prioritize
- decide what information matters
- route signals correctly
- defend communication channels

---

# 3.2 Listening Is Not Integrated Properly

## Current State

Listening currently feels:
- optional
- secondary
- unfinished
- disconnected

Examples:
- “Listening unavailable”
- placeholder-feeling interactions
- passive audio usage

---

## Problem

Listening should be:
> one of the core pillars of the game.

Not a side feature.

---

## Required Direction

Listening should become:
- distorted radio signals
- intercepted communication
- unstable Japanese fragments
- reconstruction gameplay
- shadowing systems
- active decoding

---

## Advanced Listening Ideas

### Signal Reconstruction
Player hears:
- partial sentence
- static interference
- corrupted kana

Must reconstruct:
- correct phrase
- missing word
- hidden kana

---

### Emergency Transmission
Timer-based listening challenge:
- signal degrading over time
- replay costs energy
- accuracy stabilizes channel

---

### Enemy Communication Intercepts
Player identifies:
- keywords
- intentions
- commands
- location data

using listening comprehension.

# SECTION 6 - IMMERSION BREAKERS

---

# 6.1 Terminology Inconsistency

## Current Problem

The app mixes:
- lessons
- missions
- quests
- operations
- exercises

---

## Problem

This weakens:
- immersion
- world consistency
- operational fantasy

---

## Required Fix

Define a strict hierarchy.

Example:

### Campaign
Large narrative progression

### Operations
Major mission groups

### Missions
Core gameplay stages

### Encounters
Individual learning challenges

ONLY use this hierarchy.

---

# 6.2 Flavor Text Overload

## Current Problem

Too much atmospheric wording causes:
- confusion
- interpretation fatigue
- reduced clarity

---

## Rule Needed

### 70%
Clear direct communication

### 30%
Atmospheric flavor

Not the opposite.

---

# 6.3 Fake Loading States

## Current Problem

Some transitions:
- are too long
- do not add atmosphere
- do not add function

---

## Result

The app feels:
- slower
- less responsive

---

## Required Fix

Transitions must be either:
- instant
OR
- cinematic

Never awkwardly between both.
